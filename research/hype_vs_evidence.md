# Hype vs Evidence

> `research/hype_vs_evidence.md` — separating what the market *claims* from what the evidence
> actually supports, so the recommendation rests on facts, not noise. Sources in `sources.md`.

## The core tension

The headlines say AI is everywhere in hotels; the official statistics say almost no European
hospitality firm actually deploys it. That gap is the whole decision.

| Claim circulating in the market | What the evidence shows | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| "Hotels have already adopted AI en masse (≈98%)" | Eurostat: **~6%** of EU hospitality enterprises actually deployed AI in 2023 (vs >20% in knowledge sectors); OECD: accommodation/food AI use in the low single digits | **Mostly hype** — adoption is announced, not operational |
| "AI will replace hotel staff" | The sector is *short* **~10%** of its workforce (HOTREC); hoteliers want AI to relieve teams, not cut them | **Hype / inverted** |
| "Just deploy AI and value follows" | Only **8%** of hotels have a formal AI strategy; trust (6.6/10) outruns reliance (4.7/10) — the "trust–reliance gap" | **Hype** — value needs a focused use case, not tools |
| "Back-office AI cuts invoice cost & time materially" | Manual ~€12 & ~15 min → automated ~€2–5 & ~3 min (up to ~80% lower) | **Supported** (directional, USD-derived benchmarks) |
| "What hotels actually want from AI is back-office help" | Owners use AI mainly for operational efficiency (64%); invoice processing/transaction review tops the back-office wish-list; guest-facing personalisation lags (9%) | **Supported** |
| "Now is the moment — regulation is pushing it" | ViDA adopted 2025; Belgium B2B 2026; Luxembourg law expected 2026 → domestic B2B 2028–29; LU Peppol e-invoices <100 (2021) → ~1.4 M (2024) | **Supported** |
| "Vendor X delivers 80–90% touchless / huge ROI" | Single-vendor case studies, not independent benchmarks | **Unverified** — treat as vendor claim / upper bound |

## What this means for Cleo

The credible, evidence-backed move is **not** a guest-facing AI moonshot and **not** "buy a
platform and hope." It is a **focused, human-in-the-loop back-office automation** — the one
area where (a) the cost savings are benchmarked, (b) hoteliers actually want it, (c) regulation
is creating a tailwind, and (d) the risk is low. That is exactly the selected use case, and it
argues for a **pilot** that converts these directional, mostly-non-EU benchmarks into evidence
on *this hotel's* real invoices before any scaling commitment.
