# Data Assumptions & Methodology

> `research/01_data_assumptions.md` — how the data in this repo was produced, and every
> assumption behind the numbers. Built so any figure on the dashboard can be traced to
> its origin when a sceptical CEO (or examiner) asks *"where does that come from?"*

---

## Two data layers — different nature, different rules

| Layer | Files | Nature | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Market evidence** | `data/processed/ai_adoption_evidence.csv` | **Real & cited** public figures (Eurostat, HOTREC, STATEC, KPMG, …) | Powers the Project 5 “should we invest?” dashboard. Every row carries its source and source-type. |
| **Operational** | `data/raw/*.csv` | **Synthetic** — generated by `scripts/generate_data.py` | A real hotel’s ledger and invoices cannot go in a public repo (NDA + personal data). Synthetic data is plausible, reproducible and review-safe. |

> ⚠️ **All operational data is synthetic.** It is randomly generated to be *plausible*, not
> real, and is **labelled as such everywhere**. No real company or personal data is used.
> The client’s real data is used only *off-repo*, as a reference to calibrate realism and
> to ground ROI assumptions — never ingested, never committed.

This is the standard, honest pattern for an AI-adoption case: prove the **market** with real
public data, demonstrate the **tool** with clearly-labelled synthetic data.

---

## Reproducibility

- Single generator: `scripts/generate_data.py`, fixed seed `42` → identical output every run.
- Internally consistent: the same vendors, GL accounts and dates flow through every table,
  so KPIs are coherent rather than independently random.
- Window: a rolling **12-month pilot** (June 2025 → May 2026).

---

## The operational model (what the synthetic world represents)

A single SME 5-star hotel running a pilot where AI extracts each supplier invoice and
**proposes** an accounting entry (GL account + VAT treatment + analytical allocation); a
finance team member validates before posting. An invoice is **touchless** when the AI’s
proposal is accepted with no correction.

| Table | Grain | Key fields |
|---|---|---|
| `chart_of_accounts.csv` | 1 row / GL account | account, label, department (Rooms / F&B / A&G / …) |
| `vendors.csv` | 1 row / supplier | default GL account, VAT treatment, dominant channel |
| `invoices.csv` | 1 row / invoice | net, VAT, gross, channel (pdf / email / einvoice), period |
| `ap_entries.csv` | 1 row / invoice | AI confidence, proposed vs final account, touchless, corrected, accuracy, handling minutes |
| `monthly_kpis.csv` | 1 row / month | touchless rate, accuracy, exception rate, avg minutes, hours & € saved |

## Every numeric assumption (and how to validate it)

| Assumption | Value used | Basis | Validate with client by… |
|---|---|---|---|
| Manual cost per invoice (baseline) | **€12** | Industry ~€11–15 (HighRadius/Quadient/Nanonets) — *USD-derived, indicative* | Time-and-motion on a sample of real invoices |
| Manual handling time (baseline) | **15 min** | Artsyl benchmark | Observe current AP process |
| Finance loaded hourly rate | **€45/h** | Assumption (gross + employer charges) | Confirm with HR/payroll |
| Annual invoice volume | **~500 / yr** | Sized for a single property; per-vendor means in generator | Pull actual count from the client’s AP system |
| Touchless rate trajectory | **~46% → ~74%** | Pilot-ramp assumption (model + e-invoice mix maturing) | Measure on real invoices during the pilot |
| Account-proposal accuracy | **~94%** | Modelled; e-invoices easier than scanned PDFs | Backtest AI proposals vs the validated entries |
| e-invoice share trajectory | **~26% → ~50%** | ViDA / Peppol adoption tailwind | Track actual channel mix |
| Non-deductible VAT cases | flagged, mostly **not touchless** | Hospitality reality (representation, some F&B) | Confirm VAT rules with the accountant |
| Processing surcharge / invoice | **€0.80** | Software + compute allowance | Use the chosen tool’s real pricing |

**Honest weak spots** (surfaced, not hidden): the cost and time baselines are USD-derived
global benchmarks shown as indicative euros; the loaded rate and invoice volume are
assumptions until the client confirms them. The dashboard’s opportunity calculator exposes
these as **inputs**, so the ROI re-computes the moment real figures replace the assumptions.

---

## What this means for the recommendation

Pilot-period actuals are deliberately modest (a ramp, with realistic noisy months — e.g. a
batch of non-deductible-VAT invoices lifts the exception rate). The investment case rests on
the **projection at maturity / at the client’s real volume**, computed live in the dashboard
— not on the raw pilot totals. That is the honest version of the answer.
